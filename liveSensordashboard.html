A sensor is a device that detects and responds to changes in its environment by converting physical parameters (such as temperature, pressure, humidity, or light) into signals that can be measured and recorded. Sensors are essential in modern technology, enabling automation, monitoring, and smart systems in daily life and industry.
üå°Ô∏è Temperature Sensor A temperature sensor is a device that measures the degree of hotness or coldness in its environment and converts this information into a signal that can be read by an observer or instrument. Temperature sensors are widely used in weather stations, HVAC systems, medical devices, and electronic equipment to monitor and control temperature.
